Aggregated to ET clock buckets:

| ET window | n | Hit-rate | Net |
|---|---|---|---|
| **ET 08–11 (US morning / EU overlap, UTC 12-15)** | 17 | **82.4%** | **+$0.92** |

**Interpretation:** 47% of all trades (26 of 55) fire in the ET 20-23
(UTC 00-03) Asia overnight window where the model is materially worse.
This single time bucket accounts for nearly the entire realized loss.

**Confidence:** N=17 at the strong window and N=26 at the bad window are
both substantial samples. The hit-rate spread (82.4% vs 61.5%) is real
even with sampling noise. The aggregate net delta (+$0.92 vs −$17.03 on
overlapping trade counts) is too large to be coincidence.

The current Pine Script `filter_bad_hours_utc = (9, 15, 16)` was inherited
from TradingView defaults targeting US-centric trading and does NOT cover
the actual losing hours in this market. And even where it does fire, it
only applies in phase 1 (bars 3-6) of `evaluate_entry` — phase 2 (bars
7-12) and phase 3 (bar 13+) bypass the time filter. Since most live
entries fire at bar 7+ (forced or relaxed-phase entries), the filter is
effectively inert in the current configuration.

## Finding 3: Asymmetric payoff is structural, not tunable

```
Entry price  Max gain  Max loss  Break-even hit-rate
   45¢          55¢       45¢          45%
   53¢          47¢       53¢          53%
   68¢          32¢       68¢          68%
   75¢          25¢       75¢          75%
   82¢          18¢       82¢          82%
   86¢          14¢       86¢          86%
```

9 of 13 live entries fired at 75¢+. With a 69% realized hit rate, every
trade at 75¢ has expected value ≈ `0.69 × 0.25 − 0.31 × 0.75 = −0.06`.

Why are entries this high? Pine Script's `find_atm_market` picks the
contract whose `yes_ask` is closest to 50¢ at the moment of entry. But
entries fire at bar 7+ of a 15-min cycle — by then the market has often
moved enough that no real ATM exists. The "closest to 50¢" might be 75¢.

**This is not fixable by signal improvement.** Even if hit rate climbs
to 70%, the math at 75¢ entries is still marginal. Two structural fixes:

1. Price-ceiling veto (refuse entries above 60¢)
2. Enter earlier in the cycle (bars 3-5) when ATM strikes are still ATM

Option 1 is a one-line change. Option 2 requires re-tuning the entry-phase
thresholds in `TAScoreConfig`.

## Finding 4: Conviction tiers never reach STRONG / MEDIUM in live

Tier distribution across all 21 live fills:

```
STRONG (4x): 0
MEDIUM (2x): 0
WEAK (1x):  11
MIMIC (0.5x): 10
```

The Pine Script was specifically designed for tier-scaled sizing:
- **STRONG** wins big when the model has high conviction
- **MIMIC** participates with half-size when entry is forced (bar 13+
  fallback) or relaxed-phase (bars 7-12)

Trading only WEAK + MIMIC means we're committing capital exclusively to
the bottom half of the conviction distribution. The strategy's profit
structure depended on occasional STRONG wins covering the noise on MIMIC.
Without STRONG firing, MIMIC's −EV characteristics dominate.

Two possible mechanisms:
1. Cycle position is too late — by bar 7+, EMAs and RSI have absorbed
   most of the move and `score` stays below the STRONG threshold (75).
2. BTC market conditions over the test window simply didn't produce signals
   strong enough to clear 75 on the smoothed score.

If MIMIC is responsible for the asymmetric-payoff loss, refusing MIMIC
entries shrinks coverage but improves conditional EV. Risk: on slow signal
days we may end up with 0 trades.

## Data-supported recommendations, in priority order

### 1. Time-of-day filter expansion (highest EV, ~10 LoC)

Edit `live_ta.py`'s `TAScoreConfig()` instantiation to set
`filter_bad_hours_utc = (1, 2, 3, 9, 15, 16)`. Then patch
`evaluate_entry` in `features/ta_score.py` so the time filter applies in
all three phases (currently only phase 1).

Expected impact: drops trade count from ~20/day to ~5/day. Based on the
existing data, the kept trades skew toward the +$0.92 / 82.4% hit-rate
bucket.

### 2. Price-ceiling veto (high EV, ~5 LoC)

In `live_ta.py`, refuse entries where `entry_cents > 60`. Forces the
payoff math to favor us at the moment we commit. Removes the structural
2.3× loss/win asymmetry.

Expected impact: drops trade count further; remaining trades have
favorable payoff structure.

### 3. Tier-minimum entry (refuse MIMIC)

Modify `live_ta.py` to refuse any decision with `decision.tier_name == "MIMIC"`.
MIMIC entries are forced or late-phase relaxed and the data doesn't
support them being +EV.

Risk: may produce 0 trades on slow signal days. Worth running for a few
days alongside #1 and #2 to measure.

### 4. Loss-streak halt (mechanical safety)

After N consecutive losses (e.g., N=3), pause trading for the rest of
the UTC day. Catches regime breakdowns as they happen without requiring
knowledge of WHY.

Useful even if #1 is effective, as a backstop.

### 5. Retire `mean_revert_dislocation` permanently

In the engine v2 paper trader, remove `mean_revert_dislocation` from
`tradeable_regimes`. The 0/5 hit rate is robust enough to act on.

## What the data does NOT support

- **More signal complexity.** 69.1% hit rate says the model already knows
  something. The bottleneck is selectivity, not predictive power.
- **More features.** Adding indicators won't fix the payoff asymmetry.
- **Faster execution.** The 250ms decision interval already covers most
  of the microstructure half-life envelope. Going lower would help only
  for explicit market-making, which isn't this strategy.
- **DCA / ladder as a profitability source.** The ladder shadow is the
  right experiment for understanding contract overshoot behavior, but
  even if it works perfectly, it's damage control — not an edge. It
  doesn't change the math of paying 75¢ for 25¢ payoffs.

## Sample-size caveats

- N=55 isn't huge. Confidence intervals on per-hour hit rates are wide,
  especially for hours with only 2-3 trades.
- The time-clustering effect (18× over i.i.d. on 4-loss streaks) is
  robust enough to act on tonight.
- The tier distribution (0 STRONG / 0 MEDIUM) is informative but might
  reverse during a different BTC regime. Don't lock in "model never
  hits STRONG" as a permanent assumption.
- The −$19.90 net is a single market regime over ~21 hours. A different
  regime could produce different results. We're testing one strategy
  in one market window.
